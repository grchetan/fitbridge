Struggling With Thyroid?
Eat Smart. Stay Healthy.

A practical, science-backed approach to support your thyroid with the right foods and habits.

What is the Thyroid?

The thyroid is a small, butterfly-shaped gland located in the front of your neck. It might be small, but it plays a huge role in your health. It produces hormones—T3 (triiodothyronine) and T4 (thyroxine)—that control metabolism, energy, temperature, and heart rate.

When Your Thyroid Isn’t Working Right

  • Hypothyroidism: Not enough thyroid hormone
  • Hyperthyroidism: Too much thyroid hormone
  • Hashimoto’s Thyroiditis: Autoimmune attack on thyroid
  • Goiter or nodules in the gland

Why Diet Matters for Thyroid

Diet isn’t a cure, but it helps your thyroid function better, supports medication, and boosts energy.

Best Nutrients:

  • Iodine: Seaweed, salt, fish
  • Selenium: Brazil nuts, grains, fish
  • Zinc: Lentils, seeds, eggs
  • Iron: Greens, legumes, whole grains
  • Vitamin D: Sunlight, mushrooms
  • Omega-3: Walnuts, flaxseeds, fish

Foods to Avoid

  • Goitrogens: Cabbage, broccoli, soy
  • Processed Foods: High sodium, sugar
  • Gluten: May trigger autoimmunity
  • Alcohol/Caffeine: Reduces medicine absorption

When & How to Eat

  1. Take medicine on empty stomach (30–60 mins before breakfast)
  2. Wait 30 mins before tea/coffee
  3. Eat every 3–4 hours
  4. Eat fiber-rich food
  5. Drink 2.5–3 L water daily

Lifestyle Tips

  • 7–8 hrs of sleep
  • Walk, yoga, strength workouts
  • Reduce stress
  • Check TSH, T3, T4, antibodies regularly
